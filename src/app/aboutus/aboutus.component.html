About Us

the botox course is the gold standard for neuromodulator training in Canada. For many medical professionals, the path to entering medical aesthetics is confusing, daunting and stressful. This course was built to support learners who are keen on expanding their scope of practice to include neuromodulators. We guarantee engaging lecturers, a positive learning environment and an innovative educational experience. Our team is comprised of highly motivated, personable and experienced professionals whose purpose is to equip you with the fundamental medical and business skills you need to succeed.

Meet Our Team

Dr. Andrew Dargie, BSc, MD, CCFP

EDUCATOR

Dr. Andrew Dargie is originally from Calgary, Alberta. He attended Stanford University in California where he earned a Bachelor of Science in Biology. He obtained his Medical Degree from the University of Calgary and went on to start a Vascular Surgery residency, but ultimately transferred to obtain his CCFP designation. He currently practices advanced medical aesthetics in Kelowna and Vernon, BC. He also works as an Emergency Room Physician at Penticton Regional Hospital and is the Department Head of Emergency Medicine at South Okanagan General Hospital. Dr. Dargie has a passion for teaching and is part of a team dedicated to ensuring the highest standard of neuromodulator education.

Dr. Robert Morrell, BSc, MD, DipSM

EDUCATOR

Dr. Robert Morrell, director of the Medical Rejuvenation Centre, graduated from the University of British Columbia’s Biochemistry program and then graduated from the faculty of medicine. He then continued studies in internal medicine at McGill University and later became a teaching fellow in Lyon, France. Upon academy completion, Dr. Morrell began practicing medicine in Vancouver and has continued for over 25 years. In 1999, Dr. Morrell opened his cosmetic practice and over the last decade his clinic has continued to offer the latest anti-aging and skin rejuvenating procedures.

Dr. Morrell is a spokesperson and technical trainer for the botox course and the filler course, as well as the expert course. As a member of the American Society for Laser Medicine and Surgery, Dr. Morrell is also an active national and international educator, and sat the board of directors for the Canadian Academy of Aesthetic Medicine (CAAM) advocating higher standards of care and efficacy. Committed to staying up to date on the latest technical advances in the industry, Dr. Morrell attends professional courses, such as Harvard’s Post Graduate Laser Course, Laser & Aesthetic Skin Therapy: What’s the truth? and Miami South Beach Symposium in aesthetic skin care.

Dr. Brittany Macdonald, BSc, MD, CCFP

EDUCATOR

Originally from New Brunswick, Dr. Brittany Macdonald grew up on the East Coast of Canada. After completing her Bachelor of Science degree at Dalhousie University she initially worked with expectant mothers as a researcher in epigenetics at the IWK Children’s Hospital in Nova Scotia. She later completed her medical degree in the nation’s capital at the University of Ottawa, before returning East for her residency in Family Medicine at Dalhousie University, where her training included a focus in skin disease and minor surgery. Dr. Macdonald currently practices advanced medical aesthetics in Calgary, Alberta, where she also continues to practice as a Family Physician. Dr. Macdonald is a passionate educator, and enjoys working with learners in both her medical and aesthetic practices.

Becky Wilkins, RN

EDUCATOR

Becky Wilkins has been a Registered Nurse for over 31 years. Combining her training as a registered nurse with her passion for helping people look and feel their best, allowed a transition into aesthetic medicine. Becky founded one of Alberta’s first Medical Spas over 25 years ago and now has 2 locations and a team of 30 staff. Aesthetic medicine not only requires an artistic eye, patient safety, and technical skill, but also a strong commitment to client satisfaction. Integrity and honest communication with prospective clients is Becky’s priority. She has been teaching injection techniques throughout Western Canada for the past 5 years for one of the largest pharmaceutical companies. Her style of teaching leaves students and models with the confidence to achieve very natural results performed in the most gentle and safest way.

Dr. Robert Dale, MD

Dr. Vance W. Elliot, BSc, MD, CCFP, FISHRS

EDUCATOR

Dr. Vance Elliott is from a small town in Saskatchewan. He completed a BSc in Biology at Concordia University in Edmonton, AB and went on to Medical School at the U of S. He was Chief Resident during his Family Medicine Residency Program in Regina, SK. He began practice in Hair Restoration Surgery at the conclusion of his FMR residency in 1996. In 2005 he began practice in cosmetic injectables and has since been a pioneer in Canada in the use of cannulas for fillers. He has taught basic and advanced cannula filler technique to hundreds of injectors from North America over the past 15 years. He has a particular interest in the use and teaching of Sculptra.

Brynn Fitzgerald, RN
